Quick and Easy Rice

I am by no means a master chef, the number of things I'm able to cook can probably be counted on 1 hand, I've even managed to burn water before, but here is a quick and easy way to make a perfect bowl of rice. And if the act of reading offends you, or perhaps you don't want to sully your eyes with the written word, you can find a video of this recipe by chef Alton Brown at the bottom of this page.

What you will need:


Ingredients

  • 2 Cups of Rice
  • 3 Cups of Water
  • 2 Tblsp of Butter
  • 2 Pinches of Kosher Salt

    • Cookware

  • 2-3 qt Saucepan with Lid for Rice
  • Kettle or Pot for Water
  • A Fork
  • A Spatula
  • A Timer
  • Sheet of Foil (Optional)

    • Instructions:

  • Begin by pouring 3 Cups of Water into a Kettle or a separate Pot and begin to bring the Water to a boil.
  • While the Water comes to a boil, pour 2 Cups of Rice, 2 Tblsp of Butter, and 2 Pinches of Kosher Salt into the 2-3 qt Saucepan and suaté over High heat.
  • Using a Spatula stir the Rice until the Butter has completely melted and evenly coated the Rice. The Water should be boiling by this point.
    • NOTE: If the Lid for the Saucepan does not have a tight fit on the Saucepan, stick a Sheet of Foil between the Lid and the Saucepan.
    • CAUTION: The following step will create a lot of steam.
  • Once the Rice has been thoroughly sautéed, pour the boiling Water in with the Rice, turn off the heat completely, cover with Lid and Optional Foil, then set heat to the lowest setting.
  • Wait 20 minutes.
  • Turn off the heat and remove the Lid. Using a Fork gently fluff the Rice to let out excess steam and let sit for another 2-3 minutes.
  • Enjoy your perfectly cooked Rice.
